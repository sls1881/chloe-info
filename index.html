Chloe's Dog Sitting Guide

Hello new friend—My name is Chloe and I’m a Chiweenie. At least that’s what we think I am. I’m four years old and I love to cuddle. I am a little nervous around new people, big dogs, and without my mom, but I’m excited to be your friend.

My mom says I don’t dog very well because I don’t chase balls and mostly just want to be held at the dog park, but I do like to play tug-a-war.

My favorite place to be is a warm, blanketed lap. I also love car rides. I’m too small to have the window down, but I still like to go places with you.

Stuff you should know about Chloe

  • Chloe doesn’t have any known allergies—it’s ok to give her treats
  • She grazes so you can fill her bowl and just leave food out
  • She’ll whine if she needs to go potty, but she’ll also whine if she wants you to play with her
  • She normally poops once or twice a day
  • I walk her about 2 mile in the morning and another mile in the evening to get her little legs moving
  • She likes to sleep with you but might not sleep under the covers the first few nights
  • If you bring her to work she might be a little nervous and sometimes barks at the other dogs
  • If I see a dog before she does I say “No barking” to prevent her from starting

Training and Commands

  1. Chloe is trained to walk on the right side, sometimes she wonders a little
  2. She knows sit, stay, wait, come, laydown, and high five
  3. When releasing a wait or stay command I say “All done”

These are only important if you want to use them.
